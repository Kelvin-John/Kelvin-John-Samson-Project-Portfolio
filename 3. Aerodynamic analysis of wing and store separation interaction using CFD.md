<h1>Aerodynamic analysis of wing and store separation interaction using CFD (Minor Project) </h1>

- The inspiration for this project was from a video clip of a military Biennial exercise held in India called Vayu Shakti where the Sepecat Jaguar dropped a few bombs, which triggered the thought in my brain about the aerodynamic interactions between the store and wing which I pitched to my teammates and subsequently our project guide Dr. S.Senthilkumar, Phd, though the first thing he had mentioned that this would be very computationally expensive due to the sheer interest we had excepted on a compromise of a linear drop and a wing which goes across the enclosure and save on the wing tip vortex shedding making essentially a pseudo 3D problem.
- We had initially run a 3D full mesh with dynamic mesh without setting the outer zone as deforming due to which the mesh stretch and eventually break the setup which is what the first animation attached below hence why the capture time is very low.
- Worked as a team of 3 to study the aerodynamic characteristics of store release on an airfoil in 2D.
- Used an overset dynamic mesh with a linear drop with UDFs.
- Submitted to the CFD Symposium 2026 conference awaiting response.
- Validated using a DTIC paper and a Masters thesis.
